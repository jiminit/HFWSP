The interventions are all examples only, there are many ways to modify your cardiovascular risk, and everyone's response to specific changes will vary. The ones tested here were as follows:
  • No intervention – assuming no preventive action is taken, so LDL-C stays the same and blood pressure increases with age.
  • Small LDL-C change – start eating psyllium cereal, leading to a 0.35 mmol/L reduction in LDL-C (this is an example, there are many things you can do to change your LDL-C)
  • Large LDL-C change – start taking 10 mg rosuvastatin once daily, leading to a 45.6% reduction in LDL-C
  • Achieve LDL-C target – maintain an LDL-C of 1.8 mmol/L over your lifetime
  • Small SBP change – lower your sodium intake moderately, leading to a 2 mmHg reduction in SBP
  • Large SBP change – start taking blood pressure-lowering therapy, leading to a 14.8 mmHg reduction in SBP
  • Achieve SBP target – maintain a systolic blood pressure of 120 mmHg over your lifetime

  • Quit smoking
